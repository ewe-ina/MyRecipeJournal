Hearty, aromatic spaghetti with lentils and soy mince in a rich tomato sauce 🍝

---

🔹 2 servings, 510 kcal each </br>
🔹 soak the lentils for half an hour before cooking </br>

---

#### Ingredients

&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 100 g red lentils </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 140 g wholegrain spaghetti </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 50 g soy mince </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 tbsp rapeseed oil (15 ml) </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 red onion (110 g) </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 small carrot (100 g) </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 300 g tomato passata </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 2 tsp Herbes de Provence </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> salt to taste </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> a few fresh basil leaves </br>


#### Preparation

Half an hour before cooking, pour boiling water over the lentils and let them soak.

Cook the pasta in lightly salted water. Add the soy mince to the lentils — if the lentils have absorbed all the water, add a bit more.

Heat the oil in a pan. Add the diced onion and sauté until golden. Add the grated carrot and fry briefly. Add the lentils, soy mince, and tomato passata. Mix, season with herbs and salt (careful — passata is often already salted).

If the sauce is too thick, add a little water. Heat the sauce, stirring occasionally. Remove the pan from the heat, add the cooked pasta, mix, and it’s ready.

Serve on plates and sprinkle with fresh basil (which I currently don’t have at home — but I have a much better reason than usual not to go to the store for such a tiny thing 😉).

P.S. These portions are quite generous in volume!

Enjoy! 💙
