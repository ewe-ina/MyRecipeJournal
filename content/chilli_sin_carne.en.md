#### Ingredients

&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> half a package of soy mince (~70 g) </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> boiling water </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 tbsp oil </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 red onion </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 2 garlic cloves </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 2 tomatoes </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> ½ red bell pepper </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> some water </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 can red kidney beans </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> 1 can corn </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> chilli sin/con carne spice mix (ground chili, garlic powder, ground coriander, salt, onion powder, ground cumin, sweet paprika, cinnamon, thyme) </br>
&emsp; <input type="checkbox"> lamb's lettuce </br>

#### Preparation 

Pour boiling water over the soy mince to soften and expand it.

Heat the oil in a pan. Add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until golden. Add the diced tomatoes and bell pepper. Splash in a little water and simmer covered for about 10 minutes.

Remove the lid, add the soy mince, kidney beans, drained corn, and the spice mix. Stir well, bring to a boil, and it’s ready.

Before serving, garnish with lamb’s lettuce (a delicious fresh green touch 🙂).  
You can enjoy it on its own, with bread, or with rice.
(It tastes even better the next day 😋)

Enjoy! 💙
